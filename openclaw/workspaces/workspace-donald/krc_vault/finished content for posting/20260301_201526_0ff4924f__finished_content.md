OVERLAY_HOOK_3: The shift from seeking relief to actually seeking the Redeemer.

CAPTION:
Most men repent when the pain gets bad enough.

They get on their knees, they confess, they cry. And the moment the pressure lifts, they stand back up and keep going.

That is not repentance. That is pain management.

Here is the distinction that will change everything: repentance toward God is turning away from sin because you love Him. Pain-based repentance is turning toward God because you cannot bear the weight anymore.

One is a relationship. The other is a transaction.

And God knows the difference.

You can fool your pastor. You can fool your accountability partner. You can fool the version of yourself that needs to believe you have changed. But when the motive is relief and not love, the cycle never ends. The behavior shifts slightly, the pain fades, and within weeks you are back at the same altar with the same words and the same tears.

Because you did not turn from the sin. You turned from the pain.

True repentance does not ask God to make the feeling stop. It asks God to change the man so the feeling no longer has the same grip. That is a much slower, much harder, much more honest process. And most men never start it because they keep settling for the shortcut.

The relief-seeker and the repentant man look identical on the outside for about three weeks. Then one of them falls again and one of them does not.

When the urge hits to cry out to God: pause first. Ask honestly, do I want Him, or do I want the weights off my chest? Sit with the answer for 60 seconds before you speak.
