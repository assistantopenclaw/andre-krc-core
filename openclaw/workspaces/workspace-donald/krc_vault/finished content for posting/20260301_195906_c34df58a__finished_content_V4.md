Here is the most counterintuitive truth about peace.

You are not waiting for it. You are standing in it, choosing something else.

Scripture does not describe the Garden as a place humanity was permanently locked out of after the fall. It describes a condition of constant access to God that was interrupted by a choice. The apple was not a locked door. It was a preference: I want that more than I want this.

And we repeat that choice every single day.

You wake up in a moment that has complete access to God's presence. Stillness is available. Gratitude is available. The quiet certainty that you are held is available in the next thirty seconds if you choose it.

Instead most men immediately grab the apple. The first check of the phone. The first replay of the argument. The first mental simulation of what could go wrong today before anything has even happened. The first scroll into other people's highlight reels before you have spent five minutes in your own actual life.

These are not neutral habits. Each one is a choice to leave the garden and enter the apple factory. And the apple factory is loud and fast and you can stay in it all day without ever realizing you chose to be there.

The garden did not close. You are walking out of it repeatedly and wondering why peace feels so hard to find.

This is not a rebuke. It is the most hopeful thing I know. Because if the distance was created by a choice, it can be closed by a choice. You do not need a breakthrough experience or a healed season or a resolved situation. You need a different first decision in the morning.

Before the phone. Before the replay. Before the simulation. Five minutes in the garden.

The presence is there. It was there before you woke up. Put down the apple and step back into it.
