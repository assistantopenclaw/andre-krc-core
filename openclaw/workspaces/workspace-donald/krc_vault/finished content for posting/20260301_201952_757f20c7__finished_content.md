OVERLAY_HOOK_2: The man who sets a new goal gets his engine back.
OVERLAY_HOOK_3: The dopamine-to-goal link that explains why you cannot get off the couch.

CAPTION:
You used to have energy.

You could get up early. Work late. Push through. You were moving.

Then the relationship ended, or the goal fell apart, or the life you were building stopped making sense, and now getting out of bed feels like work.

You are not broken. You are not depressed by accident. You are goalless. And your body is responding correctly.

Here is the biology: dopamine, the neurochemical responsible for drive and motivation, is not released when you achieve things. It is released as your brain tracks progress toward a meaningful goal. When the goal disappears, the dopamine disappears. And without dopamine, everything feels flat, heavy, pointless.

The problem is not your work ethic. The problem is that the goal you were running toward is gone and you have not replaced it with a new one.

For most men, that missing goal was her. The relationship was the organizing center of the motivation. Make her happy. Build a life with her. Earn her respect. When that collapsed, the engine did not have a destination anymore. So it idled.

The path back is not through more discipline. It is through a new destination. A God-centered goal that is large enough to pull you forward and real enough to demand daily progress.

Today: write down the goal you lost. Acknowledge it is gone. Then write one new God-centered milestone you can measure progress toward this week.
