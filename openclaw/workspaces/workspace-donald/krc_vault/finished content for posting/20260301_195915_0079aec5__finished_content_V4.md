CAPTION:
Nobody warns men about this particular trap.

You worked hard to love her well. You showed up consistently. You put her first. You built your entire emotional world around her wellbeing and called it devotion.

And somewhere in that devotion, something went wrong that you still cannot fully explain.

Here is what happened: you gave her God's job. Not intentionally. You did not sit down and decide to make her your source of peace, your identity, your reason for stability. It happened gradually, the way all idolatry happens, by slowly placing more and more weight on a thing until the thing was never built to carry.

No woman is built to be the foundation of a man.

When she becomes your foundation, she feels it. Not as love. As pressure. As the suffocating awareness that your entire emotional state is tethered to her. That your peace depends on whether she is okay. That if she has a hard day, or pulls back, or simply needs space, something in you destabilizes and she can feel that happen.

A woman cannot rest in a man whose stability depends on her. She will test it, even unconsciously, because she needs to know the wall will hold when she pushes. And if it does not hold, she cannot trust it to protect her from anything that actually matters.

"Monogamy to God" is the framework that changes this. It means your identity, your peace, your ground, your sense of being enough: none of it lives in her. It lives in God. Which means she can push and you stay solid. She can have a season and you are not undone by it. You love her freely because you are not desperate for her to hold you together.

That is what she is actually looking for. Not more devotion. More stability.

Redirect the monogamy. Put God in His seat. Watch what happens to the relationship when she is no longer carrying the weight of being your everything.

Today: identify one area where your peace or identity is still tethered to her response. Bring that specific attachment to God in prayer this morning and ask Him to take the seat back.
