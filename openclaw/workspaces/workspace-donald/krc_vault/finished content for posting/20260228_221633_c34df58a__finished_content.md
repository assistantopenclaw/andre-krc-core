CAPTION:
Here's a discernment tool most people never get taught.

Not all anxiety is the same. And treating it all like a problem to eliminate — or a demon to rebuke — will actually cause you to run from some of the best invitations God puts in front of you.

There are two kinds of anxiety worth knowing:

The first is fear-based anxiety. It shrinks you. It keeps you inside the circle of what's familiar and safe. It tells you to stay small, stay hidden, don't speak up, don't take the risk. It pulls you away from God and away from other people. This is the one to reject.

The second is growth anxiety. It shows up right before you do something that's actually aligned with who you're becoming — the prayer you almost didn't say, the conversation you almost didn't start, the step you almost talked yourself out of. It feels like nerves. It feels like "I don't know if I can." But it doesn't pull you toward isolation. It pulls you toward the thing God is calling you forward into.

The test is the direction.

Fear-based anxiety says: retreat. Growth anxiety says: advance.

Fear-based anxiety is about self-preservation. Growth anxiety is about expansion into something bigger than yourself.

The enemy uses the first to keep you stuck. God uses the second to stretch you into someone you've never been.

So the next time you feel anxious, don't just rebuke it. Ask the question: is this pulling me toward God or away from Him? Is this asking me to shrink or to grow?

The answer will tell you whether to push through it or cast it out.

Once you can tell the difference, anxiety stops being the enemy. It becomes the signal.
