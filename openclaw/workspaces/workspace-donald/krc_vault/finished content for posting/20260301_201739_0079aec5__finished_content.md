OVERLAY_HOOK_2: Are you building a life or building a glass house?
OVERLAY_HOOK_3: The bus-proof foundation principle: why God is the only identity that survives.

CAPTION:
Most men build their identity on their most important relationship.

Their wife. Their family. Their role as a father or a husband. And for a while it works. They feel solid. They feel like they know who they are.

Then the relationship fractures and suddenly they do not know where they are standing anymore.

That is not a coincidence. That is a structural failure.

When your identity lives inside another human being, it is only as stable as that human being. And here is the hard truth: every human being can leave, can change, can be taken. Every foundation that is built on something mortal is one accident away from total collapse.

God is the only foundation that cannot die.

You might be reading this and thinking it sounds like generic church language. It is not. This is a functional reality. When a man roots his sense of self in his relationship with Christ rather than in his relationships with people, he becomes unmovable. Not unfeeling. Not cold. Unmovable. Because the thing that holds him cannot be altered by a conversation, a court document, or a phone call.

The test is simple: what would be left of your identity if your primary human relationship ended today? If the answer is very little, your foundation needs work. That is not a shame. That is a starting point.

This week: write down every part of your identity that depends on another person being present. Then ask: which of these belongs to God?
