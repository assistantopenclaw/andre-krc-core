CAPTION:
You have been trying to stop the thoughts.

You white-knuckle through them. You rebuke them. You distract yourself, you pray over them, you replace them. And they keep coming back.

That is because the thoughts are not the problem.

The thoughts are a symptom. And you have been treating the symptom like it is the disease.

Imagine a pipe in your wall that is leaking. Water is coming through the drywall, staining everything, destroying the floor. You could stand there all day and mop the floor. You could get faster at mopping. You could get better mops. But until you go to the source of the leak, the floor stays wet.

That is what you are doing every time you fight the intrusive thought directly.

The thought is the water on the floor. The source, the broken pipe, is whatever you are actually building your life on in place of God. Could be her. Could be the relationship. Could be the fear of losing it. But something underneath that thought is still pouring. And the thought will keep coming until the source shifts.

You cannot think your way out of a source error. You can only worship your way out of one.

The moment you stop engaging with the thought and redirect your energy toward praising God, building intimacy with Him, and filling the source back up from the right well, the output begins to change. Not immediately. But it changes.

Next time the thought comes: do not argue. Label it out loud: "symptom." Then immediately move to praise. One minute. No negotiating.

Comment WALL if you refuse to be moved.
