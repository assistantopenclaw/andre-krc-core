OVERLAY_HOOK_1: When your life explodes, the wrong support system disappears.
OVERLAY_HOOK_2: The man who leads his kids through the storm first.
OVERLAY_HOOK_3: Grace-first leadership: what it looks like when a mentor actually shows up.

CAPTION:
Most men have a network that works when they are winning.

Call them when business is good, when the family is intact, when life is running clean. They show up every time.

Call them when your children are in danger and your world is on fire and you cannot think straight. Watch how many of them suddenly go quiet.

That is not community. That is an audience.

Real support does not disappear when the explosion happens. It leans in. It says: handle your family, be with your kids right now, and when you are ready to come back up for air, I am here.

Grace-first leadership looks like a man who puts the actual emergency above the agenda. Who understands that the schedule is a tool, not a master. Who knows that a man walking through a crisis needs permission to handle the most important thing first, not pressure to perform in the middle of it.

Here is what most men in crisis need and almost never get: someone who does not make them justify the emergency. Someone who simply says I have grace for what you're going through.

That is not weakness in the leader. That is exactly the character of a man who has been through enough to know that no session, no call, no agenda matters more than a father protecting his children.

If you are in a storm right now: the first step is safety. Get your kids safe. Get yourself stable. Then and only then come back to everything else.
