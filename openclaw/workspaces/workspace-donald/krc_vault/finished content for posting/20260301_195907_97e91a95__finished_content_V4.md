CAPTION:
You wouldn't hire a contractor and say "make it better."

That instruction is too vague to act on. The contractor has no idea which wall you want moved, what floor needs replacing, or what "better" actually looks like to you. So they guess. And you get back something you didn't ask for, couldn't use, and still had to pay for.

Most men pray exactly like that.

"God, give me guidance." "Help me figure this out." "Make something happen." And when a specific answer doesn't come back, they conclude the line is dead.

The line isn't dead. The question was too vague for a precise answer to land on.

God is a counselor. And every good counselor responds to the quality of what you bring in. A vague request produces a vague response. A specific question like "What exactly should I say in the next five minutes of this conversation?" or "What do I do tonight when she shuts down?" gives an answer somewhere to actually land.

Think about the difference: "God, what should I do with my life?" versus "God, what should I do tomorrow morning when the urge to reach out hits?" One is a weather forecast. The other is a surgical ask. One returns a proverb. The other returns a step.

This isn't reducing God to a task manager. It's understanding that prayer is a real conversation with a real participant, and real conversations respond to the quality of what you bring in.

You've been praying in headlines. Start praying in blueprints.

He was always ready to respond. He's waiting for a question specific enough that an answer can actually find you.
