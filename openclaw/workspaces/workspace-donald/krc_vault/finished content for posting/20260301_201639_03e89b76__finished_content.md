OVERLAY_HOOK_1: Your feelings are a terrible reason to get married.
OVERLAY_HOOK_2: The woman who stops hiding from the man she wants.
OVERLAY_HOOK_3: The handcuff principle that makes Biblical marriage work.

CAPTION:
Most people get married because they feel something.

They feel chemistry. They feel safe. They feel chosen. And when the feeling fades, they start wondering if they married the wrong person.

That is because they built a covenant on a foundation that was never designed to hold it.

Feelings are not the point of marriage. They are a side effect of something deeper.

The real point of marriage is that two people, in front of God, bind themselves permanently to each other so that truth has a safe place to live.

Think about what that actually means. Without the permanent commitment, you will never fully show your spouse your worst. You will manage yourself. You will perform. You will present the version of yourself that keeps them from leaving. Because the door is still technically open.

The moment that door closes, when you have made a vow before God that you are not walking away, something shifts. Now you can tell each other the truth about your failures, your fears, your patterns, because neither of you is going anywhere. The safety does not come from chemistry. It comes from the covenant.

That is not a cage. That is the only environment where two broken people can actually heal each other.

The handcuffs are not punishment. They are the protection that makes real love possible. And the man who understands that is not looking for a woman who makes him feel good. He is looking for a woman he can be safely bound to in front of God.

Reflect today: am I looking for a feeling, or am I looking for a woman I can be truthfully committed to in the presence of God?
