CAPTION:
Imagine a pipe in your wall has a slow leak.

Water is soaking through, warping the drywall, spreading damage. Every day you go at the wet wall with a hair dryer, soaking up the moisture, drying the surface. It looks better temporarily. Then tomorrow the wall is wet again.

You are not fixing the pipe. You are managing the symptom.

That is exactly what most men do with obsessive thought loops. They apply techniques to the surface: breathing exercises, distraction, counting days, journaling, rebuking the thought. And every one of those tools works for a moment, and then the wall is wet again by morning.

The thought is not the source. The thought is evidence of a source. Something underneath it is feeding the loop. And that source is always, at its root, a form of misplaced worship. An idol. Something you made into the home of your peace, your identity, or your sense of being enough.

When the idol is intact, the thoughts will keep coming. Not because you are weak. Because the supply line is open.

The fix is not more aggressive symptom management. The fix is finding the pipe.

Where are you placing the weight of your identity right now? Not theoretically, in the moment when the thought hits hardest, what is the thing you are afraid of losing? The answer to that question is the source. That is where the work is. Not the thought. The attachment underneath it.

When God is the actual source, the supply line to the idol gets cut. Not by willpower. By replacement. And the thought has nowhere to go because the thing feeding it is gone.

Stop drying the wall. Find the pipe and turn off the water.

Today: when the obsessive thought hits, instead of attacking it, ask yourself one question: what am I most afraid of losing right now? Sit with the answer. That is where the real work is.
