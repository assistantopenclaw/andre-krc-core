CAPTION:
Here is the version of repentance most men in pain attempt at least once.

The relationship ends. Or the season falls apart. And suddenly God becomes very real and very important. You stop the old behaviors. You start reading. You show up. You clean up. And quietly, in the back of your mind, runs the belief: if I do this right, the situation changes.

That is not surrender. That is a proposal.

You are offering God your improvement in exchange for His intervention. The repentance is genuine in its emotion. The behavior change is real. But the motive underneath is transactional: I will give You my obedience, and You will give me back what I lost.

And when the outcome does not come, something breaks. The effort did not produce the return. The deal fell through. And now God feels unreliable, or deceptive, or distant.

But God does not honor proposals. He honors the heart. And a heart that is only turning because of what it expects to receive is not yet a surrendered heart. It is a heart still in control, still trying to negotiate the terms.

Real surrender does not have a return policy. It says: whatever You have for me, I accept. Not because I will get what I want. Because You are who You say You are, and I trust that more than my own plan.

That is the moment the peace becomes permanent. Not the moment the behavior changed. The moment the motive changed.

You may have done the right things. But you cannot bribe the God who sees directly into your motive. He wants the heart behind the action, not just the action.

Today: ask yourself honestly what you were expecting to receive when you last got serious with God. Name it. Then sit with this question: are you willing to surrender that expectation and trust Him with the outcome? That conversation is where the real surrender begins.

Comment SURRENDER if you are ready to stop negotiating and give Him the whole thing.
