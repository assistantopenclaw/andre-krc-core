OVERLAY_HOOK_2: The man who knows how to navigate the turn gets back to full speed.
OVERLAY_HOOK_3: The downshift principle: why slowing down in this season is the fastest way through it.

CAPTION:
Before the crisis, you were moving.

Top gear. Clear road. High output, high momentum, clear direction. You knew who you were and where you were going and the speed felt right.

Then the turn came.

Maybe it was the relationship. Maybe the business. Maybe the season of loss you did not see coming. And now everything that was working is not working the same way and you are wondering if you wrecked.

You did not wreck. You downshifted.

Every driver knows that you do not take a sharp turn at top speed. You slow down, you find the line, you manage the curve, and then once you are through it, you accelerate back to full pace. The downshift is not a failure. It is a technique.

The men who get through hard seasons fastest are not the ones who refused to slow down. They are the ones who understood the terrain, trusted the process, and kept their eyes on the exit of the turn rather than the speed they lost entering it.

God did not break your momentum. He preserved it by protecting you from the crash that would have happened if you had tried to take that turn at full speed. The slowdown is the grace.

Your job in this season is not to manufacture old speed. Your job is to navigate the turn cleanly so you exit it with everything intact.

Acknowledge the turn today. Stop trying to hit top gear inside of it. Downshift, stabilize, and ask God: where is the straightaway from here?
