## Heart of the Call
Isaac struggles with a "mental block" that prevents him from speaking even when he feels a strong prompt to help or encourage others. Andre helps him identify this silence as a form of "lying" by omission—violating the prompt of the Holy Spirit. They trace this back to Isaac's father, who led with a "shut up and do as you're told" style and never discussed problems. Andre reframes Isaac's anxiety as the "equal and opposite force" (the Accuser) resisting God's word, proving that the pressure to stay silent is actually confirmation that what Isaac has to say is valuable.

## Key Breakthroughs
- **The Accuser's Formula**: Isaac realizes that overthinking how someone will react *before* speaking is a formula for silence, which feeds the lie that his words aren't valuable.
- **Binary Choice**: Every prompt to speak is a binary choice between giving voice to God or giving voice to the Accuser (cowardice/silence).
- **The "Compass" of Anxiety**: Andre teaches Isaac that the pressure/anxiety not to speak is actually the compass pointing toward the exact direction he *must* go.

## Actionable Replacement
Replace the "shut up and hide" internal response with a conscious check: "Is the pressure to stay silent coming from the Accuser because this word is valuable?" If yes: Trust and speak, regardless of the perceived outcome.
